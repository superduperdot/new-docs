---
title: "Get your custodial address_ids"
description: "List your internal (custodial) addresses to retrieve address_ids for transfers and balances."
---

Use the Addresses API to fetch your custodial `address_id` values. These are required for transfers and balance checks.

## Prerequisites

- Bearer token ([How to authenticate](/key-concepts/authentication))
- Your `account_id` ([How to get your account_id](/guides/how-to/get-your-account-id))

<Steps>
<Step title="List your addresses">

**GET** `https://api.brale.xyz/accounts/{account_id}/addresses`

```bash
curl --request GET \
  --url "https://api.brale.xyz/accounts/${ACCOUNT_ID}/addresses" \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer ${ACCESS_TOKEN}"
```


</Step>

<Step title="Pick the right address_id">

- Filter for `type: "internal"` to get custodial addresses managed by Brale.
- Choose an address that supports the `transfer_type` you need (e.g., `solana`, `base`, `ethereum`).
- Use the `id` field as `address_id` in transfers and balance queries.
- EVM chains (ethereum, base, polygon, arbitrum, avalanche, etc.) share one address. Non-EVM chains (solana, stellar, algorand, etc.) each get their own.

</Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
For balances, pair the `address_id` with both `transfer_type` and `value_type` in the balance endpoint.
</Tip>
